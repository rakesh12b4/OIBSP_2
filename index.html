About Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests and the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam studied aerospace engineering and physics. He worked as a scientist and engineer at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he played a significant role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle.

Abdul Kalam was widely respected for his vision, leadership, and contributions to India's scientific and technological advancements. He received several prestigious awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.

Aside from his scientific work, Kalam was an excellent orator and writer. He authored numerous books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which inspired millions of people, especially the youth, to pursue their dreams and contribute to the nation's progress.

Dr. Abdul Kalam's life and achievements continue to inspire generations, and he is fondly remembered as a humble and dedicated individual who worked tirelessly for the betterment of his country.
